Introduction to digital accessibility

Victoria Department of Education

First off, Andrew will demonstrate the NVDA screen reader on a simple demo site - to show you how screen readers navigate and interact with pages.

He will then go through one of your forms to talk about issues and pain points.

The aim is not to criticise but to offer constructive feedback.

Before I hand over, I would like to talk about how different people interact with the digital world.

People with no vision may use:
  • Screen reader software (e.g. JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, Narrator)
  • Refreshable braille devices (e.g. Stand-alone, Smart displays)
People with reduced vision may use:
  • System settings (e.g. magnification, font size, spacing, colour)
  • Browser-based page zoom (e.g. increase to 400%)
  • Increase text-size only (e.g. increase text to 200%)
  • Screen magnifier software (e.g. ZoomText)
  • Screen reader software (e.g. JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, Narrator)
People with reduced movement may use:
  • Head pointer
  • Sip and puff
  • Head switch
  • Voice recognition software (e.g. Dragon NaturallySpeaking)
People with varied learning or cognition may use:
  • ‘Text-to-speech’ software
  • ‘‘Speech-to-text’ software
  • Screen masking software (e.g. Read&Write)
  • Screen reader software (e.g. JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, Narrator)
People with reduced or no hearing may use:
  • Transcript - text version in separate document
  • Captions - synchronised text presented within media
  • Sign language translations
Disabilities are varied and complex
  • Some people have multiple disabilities or conditions.
  • Disabilities may be spectrums.
  • Some disabilities change over time.
  • Some disabilities change from day to day.
